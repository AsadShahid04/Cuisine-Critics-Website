Lasagne

The word lasagne refers to a dish made with ribbon pasta, in several layers interspersed with layers of ingredients along with sauces. Lasagne has originally originated from Italy, in the region of Emilia-Romagna! In Italy, it is common to have lasagne as a main dish in dinners during Easter and Christmas. Now brought to the States through southern Italian immigrants, it is to be served as a main dish in resteraunts. This comfort food favorite layers robust tomato sauce, ground beef and a mozzarella-ricotta cheese mixture between sheets of pasta. This baked dish is perfect for everything from busy weeknight dinners to parties and entertaining.



Ingredients

  • 1 package Anna Traditional Lasagne (1 lb)
  • 1 lb Ground Beef
  • 1 jar Cento All Purpose Pasta Sauce (26 oz)
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1 Egg, beaten
  • 1 lb Ricotta Cheese
  • 8 oz Mozzarella Cheese, grated
  • 3/4 cup Parmesan Cheese, grated
  • 1 tbsp Cento Parsley
  • Cento Fine Sicilian Sea Salt, to taste
  • Cento Ground Black Pepper, to taste


Recipe

  1. 1. Cook sausage, ground beef, onion, and garlic over medium heat until well browned. Stir with crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, tomato sauce, and water.

  2. 2.Cook lasagna noodles in boiling water for a couple minutes.

  3. 3. Combine ricotta cheese with egg, parsley, and salt.

  4. 4. Spread 1 1/2 cups of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Arrange 6 noodles lengthwise over. Top with a third of mozzarella cheese. slices. Spoon 1 1/2 cups meat sauce over mozzarella, and sprinkle with 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese.

  5. 5. Bake in preheated oven for 25 minutes.
