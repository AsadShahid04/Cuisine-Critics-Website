Shirley Temples

Shirley Temple is a non-alcoholic drink made from ginger ale, grenadine, and maraschino cherry. It is a drink intended for consumption by children to avoid giving them real alcoholic drinks. Originally as a mocktail, this simple, delicious, cold pink bubbly drink that is enjoyed by youngsters and adults, and a great way to take a plain can of soda and dress it up to make it look and taste fancy. It’s the perfect holiday drink, since everyone can enjoy it.


Namesake

Shirley Temple Black was one of the most famous child actresses in the 1930s, and as she attended parties with older celebrities could not consume the same drinks as they could. In response, a mocktail derived from children-friendly ingredients was crafted for her. The drink became synonymous with her name, and the rest is history.


While Shirley Temple is a drink primarily consumed by children, adults often make them for home use or for parties. It is a safe beverage without alcohol, both delicious and safer than alcoholic drinks.


Ingredients

  • 1 ounce of grenadine
  • 4 ounces ginger ale
  • 1 Maraschino cherry


Recipe

  1. 1. Fill a glass with ice

  2. 2. Add about 1 ounce (2 tablespoons) of grenadine

  3. 3. Top with white soda, then garnish with maraschino cherries.
