Assam Laksa

This dish has such an alluring combination of salty, spicy and sweet, that you’re sure to be hooked as soon as you try it. The pungent, sour, and fiery hot noodle dish with fresh vegetables are bathed in a fish broth. Known simply as Laksa in Penang, it’s found pretty much everywhere from coffee shops to roadside stalls. It is said by many to be the most successful explosively delicious, addictive, and appetizing Nyonya concoctions. Asam in Bahasa/Malay word translates to tamarind, commonly used to give this stock its sour flavor. Asam Gelugur/ Asam Keping (dried Tamarind Peel) can be added for extra tartness. Asam laksa has a very complex flavor of sweet, sour, spicy, and umami. The lemony umami-ish aroma comes from bunga kantan (torch ginger), fish-based stock, shrimp paste, and a mixture of herbs like lemongrass.



Ingredients

  • 1 kg whole fish
  • 3 slices asam keping (optional; see note)
  • 4 Vietnamese mint sprigs
  • 4 slices galangal
  • 150 gm tamarind pulp, soaked in 1 cup hot water
  • 1 tbsp caster sugar, or to taste
  • 400 gm dried laksa noodles, softened in cold water for 6-8 minutes

Recipe

  1. 1. Clean the fish. In a pot, bring 8 cups of water to boil. Add in the fish and boil for about 10 minutes. Transfer the cooked fish out into a bowl and let cool. Strain the fish stock, and continue to boil in low heat.

  2. 2. Break the fish meat into tiny pieces and put the fish back into the stock, cover the lid, and lower the heat.

  3. 3. Grind the spice paste until fine

  4. 4. Strain the tamarind juice and keep the seed

  5. 5. In a serving bowl, add in the laksa noodles and garnish all vegetables on top. Pour the fish broth into the bowl
