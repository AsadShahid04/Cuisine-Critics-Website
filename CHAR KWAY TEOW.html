CHAR KWAY TEOW

A stir fried Chinese-inspired rice noodle dish from Maritime Southeast Asia, Char Kway Teow is a dish you need to try! In Hokkien, Char means “stir-fried” and kway teow refers to flat rice noodles. In Penang, it is commonly served on a piece of banana leaf on a plate, which is intended to enhance the aroma of the dish. This dish has such an alluring combination of salty, spicy and sweet, this dish is something anyone would easily be hooked to! No wonder it is one of the most epic noodle dishes in the streets of Malaysia! The pungent, sour, and fiery hot noodle dish with fresh vegetables are bathed in a fish broth. Known simply as Laksa in Penang, it’s found pretty much everywhere! Many people say that it is a dish whose taste can only be found in Malaysia!

This plate used to be sold by fishermen back in the day, where farmers and cockle-gatherers who doubled as char kway teow hawkers in the evening used it to help support their income. This dish was a cheap yet excellent source of energy and nutrients for the low-income.



Ingredients

  • 1 kg whole fish
  • 3 slices asam keping (optional; see note)
  • 4 Vietnamese mint sprigs
  • 4 slices galangal
  • 150 gm tamarind pulp, soaked in 1 cup hot water
  • 1 tbsp caster sugar, or to taste
  • 400 gm dried laksa noodles, softened in cold water for 6-8 minutes


Recipe

  1. 1. Grind the chili paste. Heat up a wok with 1 teaspoon oil and stir-fry your chili paste

  2. 2. Add 2 tablespoons oil/lard into the wok and add half the portion of chopped garlic into the wok and do a quick stir.

  3. 3. Transfer 6 prawn out of water and half the sausage slices into the wok.

  4. 4. Add half the bean sprouts into the wok. Immediately follow by 8 oz. or half portion of the flat noodles.

  5. 5. Add 2 1/2 tablespoons of the sauce into the wok and stir

  6. 6. Add about 1/2 tablespoon of chili paste

  7. 7. Add chives, do a couple of quick stirs
