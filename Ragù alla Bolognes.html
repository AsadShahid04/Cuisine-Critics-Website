Ragù alla Bolognes

Bolognese sauce originates from the city of Bologna. It is one of the most common foods Italy. It is even considered to be the national dish of Italy and it's Bolognese sauce is made from tomatoes, minced beef, garlic, and herbs. For the Bologna’s people, this dish is a very serious matter, even more than can be expected! The preparation of this great recipe is easy yet it needs much time, the right ingredients and a very good thick-bottomed heavy pot.



Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped (about 1 1/2 cups)
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped (about 1 cup)
  • 2 carrots, peeled, finely chopped (about 3/4 cup)
  • 6 oz. ground beef (85% lean)
  • 6 oz. ground veal
  • 3 oz. thinly sliced pancetta, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup dry red wine
  • 3 cups (about) beef stock or chicken stock, divided
  • 3 Tbsp. tomato paste
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 lb. tagliatelle or fettuccine (preferably fresh egg)
  • Finely grated Parmesan (for serving)


Recipe

  1. 1.Spread the bread crumbs on a large rimmed baking sheet and drizzle with 1 tablespoon of oil; toss well. Bake for 8 minutes. Transfer the bread crumbs to a medium bowl and toss with the basil and cheese.

  2. 2. Brush the eggplant slices with oil and season with salt and pepper. Grill over moderate heat. Transfer the eggplant to a platter, fanning them out. Brush the tomatoes with oil and season with salt and pepper. Grill over moderate heat.

  3. 3.Arrange the tomato slices over the eggplant and drizzle with oil. Sprinkle the bread crumbs over the top and serve right away.
