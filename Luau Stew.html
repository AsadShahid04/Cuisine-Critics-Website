Luau Stew

Eat this dish with rice or poi! You can find luau stew in many versions, such as plain/vegetarian, with squid, chicken, beef, or pork. Cooked until it reaches that perfect melt-in-your-mouth tenderness, and then seasoned with a dash of Hawaiian sea salt. The most popular of luau stews is made with beef brisket, but you can find various versions throughout Hawaii. It contains ginger, onion, coconut milk, and pepper to spice up this flavorful dish even more.


Ingredients

  • 2 bunch of Luau Leaves (leaves from the Taro Plant)
  • 2 bunch of stems of the Luau Leaves
  • 2 bunch of Hawaiian Salt or Sea Salt to taste
  • 1 Tbsp of minced Garlic
  • 1/2-3/4 cups of Granulated Chicken Bouillon
  • 1/2-3/4 cups of Water


Recipe

  1. 1. Prepare the luau leaves. Wash the leaves very thoroughly.

  2. 2. Mix cubes of pork shoulder with salt

  3. 3. Add oil, and turn to medium-high heat. Add the pork in a single pot.

  4. 4. Add the sliced onions and minced ginger to the pot

  5. 5. Put all the luau leaves over the onions and ginger. Bring to boil. Stir every 15 mins

  6. 6.Season with salt and add shoyu if you please!
