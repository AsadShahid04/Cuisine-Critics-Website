Mast o Khiar

Mast o Khiar is a light and refreshing dip made of yogurt and cucumber. It is a healthy alternate, and can generally be served along side rice and meat, or any bread. This is best served when it is cold, but can also be served just after being mixed up if time is limited to put the dish in the refrigerator. A very versatile dish, Mast o Khiar can be ideally served as an appetizer as a side of chips and pita bread, or as a sauce to main dishes. One of the beauties of this dish is that it only requires yogurt and cucumber! Seasoned with salt and pepper, and maybe a bit of dried mint, your dip is ready to be dipped into!



Ingredients

  • ¼ cup black or golden raisins
  • 3 Persian cucumbers (about 1/2 pound)
  • 24 ounces Greek yogurt or labneh (3 cups)
  • 2 tablespoons any combination of finely chopped fresh parsley, cilantro, basil, tarragon or dill
  • 1 garlic clove, finely grated or pounded into a smooth paste with a pinch of salt
  • Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • ¾ teaspoon ground or crumbled dried rose petals (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dried mint
  • 1 teaspoon dried dill
  • 3 tablespoons roughly chopped toasted walnut pieces


Recipe

  1. 1. Submerge the raisins in boiling water to plump for 10 minutes, then drain well.

  2. 2. Dice cucumbers into 1/4-inch pieces & place a large bowl with raisins, yogurt, fresh herbs, garlic, 3/4 teaspoon salt, 1/2 teaspoon pepper

  3. 3. Stir in the walnuts and transfer to serving bowl.
